About Us

Who we are?

We are a group of scientists, engineers, and entrepreneurs, who believe the Caribbean has high potential for development through the innovation of space technologies. We call this initiative PRatian LLC as we are the first young Caribbean professionals to reach Mars and beyond. Our mission is to promote a culture of research towards innovation and commercialization of STEM developments with a non-restricted focus on space sciences. We aim to develop scientific projects for the well-being of society, ameliorating economic growth by creating products with high commercial value and strengthening worldwide connections within the scientific community. Our ultimate goal is to obtain scientific exposure internationally and design technologies to be commercialized. We are determined for Puerto Rico to be the founding location and headquarters of space research in the Caribbean. On this website, you will be able to keep up to date with our events such as our most recent research proposals, conferences and competitions attended, recognitions and awards granted, seminars to local communities conducted, and so on.

Space Policy

Project #5 - Spring 2017

Creation of Puerto Rican space policies to identify ideal country resources and geographical locations used for research and development of space technologies. Such policies will help structure the space market in the Caribbean and attract investors to Puerto Rico.

Carlos A. Estévez-Galarza

Space Research Director

Bachelor's of Science in Theoretical Physics, minor in Applied Mathematics
University of Puerto Rico at Mayagüez Campus

Estévez has worked at the Arecibo Observatory as a planetary science researcher. Currently, he analyzes data from MSL’s Curiosity rover, specifically from its REMS station. The MSL’s REMS was recently used to suggest the possibility of liquid water formation via deliquescence of perchlorate salts during the Martian night (Martín-Torres et al., 2015). During his time in Arecibo, he has learned that planetary science is all about understanding our world better by comparing it with other worlds. I think most of the people ignore science because its apparent difficulty scares them off, but somehow planetary science gets to people. It’s something people can visualize and feel identified with. I truly believe that planetary science can give everyone the same feeling I perceived while doing my research. Through PRatian, I can bring this message to more human beings, especially to underrepresented communities in science such as Puerto Rico. Estévez believes on building a space sciences research center to be devoted to the advancement of science and education in Puerto Rico, as well as a tool to grow our economy.
